For The Grind

Streetwear clothing brand built around collection drops

iOS & Android Design

For The Grind is a streetwear clothing brand idea based on the Supreme model of releasing exclusive collection drops at given times. In this project, I conducted competitive research, mapped out a user flow, created wireframes and conducted user testing to ultimately create the prototype you see before you. The project was originally designed to show the differences between Human Interface Guidelines and Material Design Guidelines.

My Process

Objective

The goal of this project was to showcase the differences between the designs for iOS and Android. This was my first introduction to designing apps specifically using the Human Interface Guidelines and Material Design Guidelines and it helped me realize all the differences between the two systems. Starting a clothing brand is a business goal of mine, so I thought this project would be a perfect opportunity to design an app that I could use in the future.

Ideation

To get familiar with how other clothing apps are structured, I downloaded a couple of different brands' apps and walked through the user experience process of browsing through a collection, adding an item to cart, and then completing a purchase. I specifically based my design around Nike and Adidas' apps since their apps are really well designed and thought out compared to a lot of other clothing brands that mainly focus on web. I noted down the differences and similarities between the Nike and Adidas' apps and the overall customer journey and used it as a guide for mapping out my own user flow. My user flow focuses on a new user signing in for the first time, to browsing and adding items to cart, to completing a purchase after putting in credit card information. After I had this user flow mapped out, I began my low fidelity sketches. I looked to the Nike and Adidas apps for inspiration and took elements from each that I then incorporated into my own design.

Wireframes

Using the user flow and other apps as inspiration, I then created low fidelity wireframes of some of the crucial screens within the user flow. I started off designing the iOS versions since I have an Apple phone and am more familiar with iOS app design. User Testing

After getting feedback from my mentor, I went on to create high fidelity wireframes and created a prototype for both iOS and Android on InVision. I then asked a few other UI design students to test my prototypes and provide me feedback. Using the feedback I received from them, I then edited my wireframes.
